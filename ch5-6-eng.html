Working With the Text (Page 62)

Answer the following questions given below:

Note: We are providing four alternate answers of each questions so don't be confused. Also, "★" refers to our recommended answer out of the four.

A. Put these sentences from the story in the right order and write them out in a paragraph. Don’t refer to the text.

1. I shall be so glad when today is over.

2. Having a leg tied up and hopping about on a crutch is almost fun, I guess.

3. I don’t think I’ll mind being deaf for a day—at least not much.

4. But being blind is so frightening.

5. Only you must tell me about things.

6. Let’s go for a little walk.

7. The other bad days can’t be half as bad as this.

Answer: Let’s go for a little walk. Only you must tell me about things. I shall be so glad when today is over. The other bad days can’t be half as bad as this. Having a leg tied up and hopping about on a crutch is almost fun, I guess. I don’t think I’ll mind being deaf for a day, at least not much. But being blind is so frightening.

B. Answer the following questions:

1. Why do you think the writer visited Miss Beam’s school?

Answer 1: The writer visited Miss Beam's school to research and document innovative educational practices for children with disabilities.

or

Answer 2: The writer visited Miss Beam's school to understand the impact of inclusive education on the overall development of children.

or

Answer 3: The writer visited Miss Beam's school to highlight the importance of equal opportunities and inclusive education for children with diverse abilities.

or

Answer 4: The writer visited Miss Beam's school to raise awareness about the challenges faced by children with disabilities and the importance of creating an inclusive society.

2. What was the ‘game’ that every child in the school had to play?

Answer 1: The 'game' that every child in the school had to play involved experiencing simulated disabilities to develop empathy and understanding.

or

Answer 2: The 'game' that every child in the school had to play was a role-playing activity that allowed them to step into the shoes of individuals with different disabilities.

or

Answer 3: The 'game' that every child in the school had to play was a unique educational exercise aimed at fostering empathy and raising awareness about the challenges faced by individuals with disabilities.

or

Answer 4: The 'game' that every child in the school had to play involved experiencing firsthand the daily obstacles encountered by individuals with disabilities.

3. “Each term every child has one blind day, one lame day …”. Complete the line. Which day was the hardest? Why was it the hardest?

Answer 1: “Each term every child has one blind day, one lame day, one dumb day, and one day with each of your senses sharpened.” The dumb day was considered the hardest as it challenged the children's ability to express themselves without relying on spoken words.

or

Answer 2: “Each term every child has one blind day, one lame day, one dumb day, and one day with each of your senses sharpened.” The blind day was the hardest as it deprived the children of their primary sense, making navigation and communication more challenging.

or

Answer 3: “Each term every child has one blind day, one lame day, one dumb day, and one day with each of your senses sharpened.” The lame day was the hardest as it restricted the children's physical mobility and required them to adapt to new ways of movement.

or

Answer 4: “Each term every child has one blind day, one lame day, one dumb day, and one day with each of your senses sharpened.” The day with each of the senses sharpened was the hardest as it overwhelmed the children with heightened sensory experiences, making it challenging to focus and concentrate.

4. What was the purpose of these special days?

Answer 1: The purpose of these special days was to cultivate empathy, understanding, and respect among the students towards individuals with disabilities.

or

Answer 2: The purpose of these special days was to create a supportive and inclusive learning environment where children could experience and appreciate the challenges faced by individuals with disabilities.

or

Answer 3: The purpose of these special days was to break stereotypes and foster a culture of acceptance and inclusion within the school community.

or

Answer 4: The purpose of these special days was to encourage children to develop a deeper understanding of diversity, promote empathy, and dismantle barriers faced by individuals with disabilities in society.
