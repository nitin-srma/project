Working With the Text (Page 34)

Answer the following questions given below:

Note: We are providing four alternate answers of each questions so don't be confused. Also, "★" refers to our recommended answer out of the four.

1. Why did Taro run in the direction of the stream?

Answer 1: Taro ran in the direction of the stream because he wanted to quench his thirst and drink fresh water.

or

Answer 2: Taro ran towards the stream because he heard the soothing sound of water and was attracted to it.

or

Answer 3: Taro ran in the direction of the stream because he was curious to explore and see where the water was flowing.

or

Answer 4: Taro ran towards the stream because he had a feeling that there might be something interesting or valuable near the water source.

2. How did Taro’s father show his happiness after drinking sake?

Answer 1: Taro's father showed his happiness after drinking sake by bursting into laughter and wearing a wide smile on his face.

or

Answer 2: After drinking sake, Taro's father expressed his happiness by singing joyfully and dancing with graceful movements.

or

Answer 3: Taro's father displayed his happiness after drinking sake through a burst of energy, playfully engaging in lively conversations and activities.

or

Answer 4: Taro's father demonstrated his happiness after drinking sake by embracing everyone around him, radiating warmth and spreading infectious laughter.

3. Why did the waterfall give Taro sake and others water?

Answer 1: The waterfall gave Taro sake and others water as a reward for his kind and selfless actions towards the old man, showing gratitude for his compassionate nature.

or

Answer 2: The waterfall gave Taro sake and others water because it recognized his pure heart and noble intentions, granting him a special gift as a symbol of appreciation.

or

Answer 3: Taro's sincerity and genuine concern for the old man touched the waterfall's spirit, prompting it to bless him with sake as a token of recognition and honor.

or

Answer 4: The waterfall gave Taro sake and others water as a gesture of acknowledgment for his courage and determination to help the old man, showcasing the power of compassion.

4. Why did the villagers want to drown Taro?

Answer 1: The villagers wanted to drown Taro out of fear and misunderstanding, believing that sacrificing him to the water would bring prosperity and prevent further misfortunes.

or

Answer 2: The villagers, driven by superstition and desperation, believed that sacrificing Taro to the water would appease the spirits and protect the village from calamity.

or

Answer 3: Taro's refusal to comply with the villagers' beliefs and traditions threatened their established order, leading them to seek his drowning as a means of preserving their customs.

or

Answer 4: The villagers, influenced by ignorance and mob mentality, saw Taro as a scapegoat and believed that sacrificing him to the water would bring an end to their perceived misfortunes.

5. Why did the Emperor reward Taro?

Answer 1: The Emperor rewarded Taro for his bravery and compassion in saving the old man, recognizing his exceptional act of heroism and setting an example for others.

or

Answer 2: The Emperor was moved by Taro's selflessness and courage, and as a gesture of appreciation, he rewarded Taro for his noble deed, acknowledging his exceptional character.

or

Answer 3: Taro's honorable actions caught the attention of the Emperor, prompting him to bestow a reward in recognition of Taro's exemplary behavior and benevolence.

or

Answer 4: The Emperor was impressed by Taro's extraordinary act of kindness and saw it as a reflection of the virtues he valued, leading him to grant a reward to honor Taro's noble actions.
